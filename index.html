    • Recent Projects

    I have a mixed background including mechanics, electronic devices, robotics and intelligent systems. The fowllowings are some projects I have done in recent years.

  • Wearable ultrasonic elastography based on soft probe

    • This project was supervised by Prof. Sheng Xu at UC San Diego and the main cooperators are Hongjie Hu, Hao Huang and Mohan Li. My main contributions were developing the post-processing code for elastography, optimizing the probe design by simulation and experiment, and conducted demontrative experiments. A real-time wearable ultrasound system was demonstrated with ability to map the strain/modulous distribution of tissue 4-5 cm under the skin.

  • The effect of arterial stiffness on the accuracy of cuff-based blood pressure measurement

    • Extreme Mechanics Letters

    This project was supervised by Prof. Xue Feng and Prof. Yinji Ma at Tsinghua University. We Proposed a theoretical model to study the impact of arterial wall on the measured blood pressur, which was in accordance with our the simulation results of ABAQUS and the experiment results from liturature. The paper revealed that the blood pressure of arteriosclerostic patients cound be overestimated, which may help to improve the accuracy of cuff-based blood pressure measurement in future.

  • A face-tracking camera with one DOF

    •   

    This project was a simple practice using an Arduino board, a logitech camera and a stepper motor. I and Zhuyou Hu designed and completed the project. We realized face recognition using Python, with which we further control the motor to follow the movement of face and make the face stay in the center of footage.

  • Music Factory: A harp robot and Human-machine Interaction

    • This project was supervised by Prof. Gangtie Zheng at Tsinghua University and the main cooperators are Yupu Lu, Yijie Wu and Shijie Zhu. Based on a 32-channel automatic harp, we designed a layered modular framework integrating both hardware and software for implementing real-time control protocol and machine learning algorithms, which may bring more expressiveness for human-machine collaborative music performances.

  • Optimization of neck mechanism

    • This project aims to optimize the neck mechanism of a robot to realize a high degree of freedeom and a high speed as well as visulizing/simulating the movement of the neck mechanism in real time. The project was done at Purdue Univerisity under the supervision of Prof. Voyles.
